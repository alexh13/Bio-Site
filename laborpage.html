Combat Boots to Steel Toe Boots

January 09, 2019

After finishing my enlistment in the United States Army, I joined a Construction Union Hall. I spent the next 18 months working all over the Bay Area doing anything from working on refineries, like Chevron and Tesoro, to pouring concrete in 100+ degree weather at Anheuser Busch in Fairfield. I am most proud of the Anheuser Busch project - I worked on a team that installed six massive tanks for brew production. There's something about seeing my hard work take shape and others benefiting from it that makes me feel fulfilled by the short time I spent in construction. That work-satisfaction feeling is another reason I have taken a strong liking to programming. Construction and programming are the same in a lot of ways; like taking basic ideas and following the rules to create something for the benefit of others. However, I am more inclined to programming, since I am able to create for others without paying the price of my health!
